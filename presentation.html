COVID-19 hospitalizations in Emilia-Romagna, Italy

Objective

The goal of the project is computing short-term forecasts of COVID-19 hospitalizations in the Italian region Emilia-Romagna during the period from September 2021 to March 2023.

The predictions are computed weekly (\(Q=7\)), starting from Wednesdays, using data from the last \(W=21\) days. As covariates, we consider:

  • day of the week
  • if it improves the BIC, the number of positive cases (smoothed) with lag 7 or 10.

Model

We evaluated multiple models:

  • INGARCH models: stationary, linear, quadratic, and cubic
  • flexible GLM with cubic splines of the time as covariate
  • Generalized Additive Model (GAM) with a smooth function of time
  • Bayesian Structural Time Series (BSTS) with linear and seasonal trends
  • Artificial Neural Network (ANN) with 2 hidden layers and 10 repetitions

Finally, we selected the models with the best overall performance across the whole period: GAM, BSTS, ANN. These model contribute to the Ensemble, with weights that vary for each prediction window according to their performance in the previous period.
